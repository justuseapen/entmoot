## Objection Handling

### "Too expensive right now"

**Response** (in FAQ or follow-up):
```
I get it. $300 upfront or even $25/month matters.

Here's how I think about it:

If Entmoot saves your family 1 hour per week (fewer arguments, better coordination, clearer priorities), that's 52 hours per year.

What's an hour worth to you? For most families, way more than $25/month.

But only you can decide if it's worth it for your situation.
```

---

### "What if you shut down?"

**Response**:
```
Fair concern for any early-stage product.

Transparency: We're backed by investors, we have 18 months of runway, and we're growing steadily.

Our goal is to be around for decades, not months.

That said, if we ever did shut down, we'd give you 90 days notice and help you export all your data.

But I don't think you'll need that. We're just getting started.
```

---

### "I can just use free tools"

**Response**:
```
You absolutely can.

There are free calendars, free to-do lists, free goal trackers.

Entmoot's value isn't that we do things you can't do elsewhere.

It's that we connect them all together in one system designed for families.

If you're happy stitching together 5 free tools, no judgment. That can work.

But if you want one integrated system that actually makes family planning easier, Entmoot is worth trying.
```

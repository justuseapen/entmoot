## The Most Important Habit: Weekly Reviews

Daily planning is great. But weekly reviews? That's where the magic happens.

Here's why:

**Daily planning** tells you what to do today.
**Weekly reviews** tell you what's actually working.

Most families skip the review. They just keep planning, hoping something sticks.

Then they wonder why nothing changes.

---

## Your Weekly Review Template

Every Sunday (or your chosen day), spend 15 minutes on this:

### Part 1: Reflection (5 minutes)

Ask yourself (and your family):

**What went well this week?**
- Which tasks felt easy?
- What progress made you proud?
- What small win deserves celebration?

**What was challenging?**
- Which commitments were hardest to keep?
- What obstacles came up?
- What drained your energy?

**What did you learn?**
- About your goals?
- About your schedule?
- About what matters most?

Write it down in Entmoot (tap "Weekly Review" â†’ "Reflections").

---

### Part 2: Celebration (3 minutes)

Look at your wins:

- View your completed tasks (tap "Completed")
- See your goal progress (tap "Goals")
- Notice your family's achievements

This isn't optional fluff. Celebrating progress releases dopamine and reinforces the behavior.

You're literally rewiring your brain to love planning.

---

### Part 3: Planning Ahead (7 minutes)

Now look forward:

**Review next week's commitments**:
- What's already scheduled?
- What deadlines are coming?
- What family events matter?

**Adjust goals if needed**:
- Is your weekly goal too ambitious? Scale back.
- Too easy? Level up.
- Totally off track? Pause it and pick a more relevant goal.

**Assign key tasks**:
- What 3-5 things MUST happen next week?
- Who's responsible for what?
- Where's the time coming from?

## Pro Tips from Power Users

**Tip #1**: Pick the same day and time every week (Sunday 7pm, Friday 5pm, etc.). Consistency is everything.

**Tip #2**: Do it as a family when possible. Even 10-year-olds can participate.

**Tip #3**: Use the voice memo feature if writing feels tedious. Reflection is reflection.

**Tip #4**: Don't skip it even when you had a "bad" week. Especially then - that's when you learn the most.

**Tip #5**: Keep reviews short. 15 minutes max. Longer reviews don't stick.
